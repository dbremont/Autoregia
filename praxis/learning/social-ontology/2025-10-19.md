# Structure of a Social Ontology

> A social ontology has to take into account ontic - and non ontic elements.

- Agent: ...
  - Action ; Interaction - Joint Action; Expectation; Behavior; Action Set.
  - Perception
  - Interpretatction
  - Internal Cognition
  - Internal Cogntige State
  - Feedback & Reflection & Change  
  - Property: Power, ...
- Social Field
    - Material Environment; Feedback
    - Material Objects - That Mediates Joint Action - ETC.
    - Agent Set
      -  Properties of the some subset:  Joint Cognition, ...
- Property
- State: Agent, Social Field, ...
- Dynamics: How the entities are changing - how the fiels is changing overall?
