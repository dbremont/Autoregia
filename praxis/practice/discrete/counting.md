> During the last 1000 years, the reader had an ancestor A such that there was a person P who was an ancestor of both the father and the mother of A.

> A Busy airport sees 1500 takeoffs per day. Prove that there are two planes that must take off within a minute of each other.

> Find all triples of positive integers $a < b < c$ for which $\frac{1}{a} + \frac{1}{b} + \frac{1}{c} = 1$ holds.

> We have distributed 200 balls into 100 boxes, with the restriction that each box contains at least one ball and at most 100 balls. Prove that it is possible to find a selection of boxes whose total number of balls is exactly 100.

> Last year, the Division One basketball teams each played against an average of eighteen different opponents. Prove that it is possible to find a group of teams such that each team in the group has played against at least ten other teams within the group.

> The set consists of 2003 integers, none of which has a prime divisor greater than 24. Prove that L
contains four elements whose product is equal to the fourth power of an integer.

> The power sum of one hundred given real numbers is zero.  Prove that at least 99 of the pairwise sums of these hundred numbers are non-negative. Is this result the best possible one?

> There are four heaps of stones in our backyard. We rearrenge them into five heaps. Prove that at least two stones are palced into a smaller heap.

> The set of all positive integers is partitioned into several arithmetic progressions. Show that there is at least one among these arithmetic progressions whose initial term is divisible by it's diference.

> How many integers from 1 to 100 are divisible by 2, 3, or 5?

> Divide 10 students into two groups of 4 and 6. How many ways can this be done?

> In a class of 12 students, how many ways can we select a president, a vice president, and a secretary if order does not matter?

> How many ways are there to choose 5 numbers from 1 to 20 such that no two are consecutive?

> Amoeba population: There is one amoeba in a pond. After every minute the amoeba may die, stay the same, split into two, or split into three with equal probability. All its offspring, if it has any, will behave the same (and independent of other amoebas). What is the probability the amoeba population will die out?
