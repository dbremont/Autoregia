Deep-work blocks are not being defended

Work · deviation · enacted · 2026-06-24 · feeds: pps, pwos

The intention was four protected deep-work blocks. The outcome was interruptions on four of five days. This deliberation traces the cause and enacts a correction.

Observation What was noticed

Of five scheduled morning blocks (PWOS), only one ran uninterrupted. Three were fragmented by incoming messages answered "on the way past"; one was deferred entirely to absorb a smaller task. The PWOS analytics flagged the week as below the focus goal.

Deliberation Why it happened

The blocks were scheduled but not defended: nothing in the policy set states that a committed block is binding against incoming requests, so the path of least resistance — reply now, defer the block — won every time. The failure is structural, not motivational: the commitment had a calendar entry but no policy backing.

Adaptation The change

Make the commitment structural. Amend the Learning Policy to state that a scheduled deep-work block is binding and is rescheduled, not nibbled; register a routine in PWOS that closes messaging during the block.

Adaptation → enacted 2026-06-26

Learning Policy §3 amended: a committed deep-work block is binding and is rescheduled rather than interrupted. PWOS routine close-messaging registered against the morning blocks.

Follow-up

Re-open if next week's analytics still show fragmentation after the policy backing is in place. A null result here would mean the cause was not policy but something deeper (notification design, sleep) — a separate deliberation.
