Retrospective — building PRAS

Work · retrospective · concluded · 2026-06-30 · feeds: prs

A look back at constructing this system — the first piece of the loop's feedback arc to be materialized as a tool. The model worked; the lifecycle is deliberately incomplete.

Observation What was built

PRAS was implemented as a corpus of HTML deliberations indexed by a small server, on the same documents-as-data model as the Policy System. The deliberation — observation → deliberation → adaptation — is the atomic unit; the lifecycle open → concluded → enacted is expressed in document metadata.

Deliberation What the model bought

  • Traceability. Every change now has reasoning attached to it, dated and reviewable — adaptation instead of drift.
  • Decoupling. Deliberations declare their destination (PPS, AOOS, …) without hard-coupling the systems. The "feeds the policy system" relationship is a field, not a wire.
  • Honesty. The lifecycle forces a deliberation to close by enacting or explicitly declining — drift has to be named.

Adaptation The cost, and what is next

The prototype is read / browse / search. Lifecycle transitions are edits to a document's metadata, and enactment into PPS or AOOS is a manual cross-link, not an event. The cost is that the loop does not yet close itself: a concluded deliberation does not emit anything another system can hear.

Adaptation → concluded (filed; future work named)

The next step is to make enactment an event on the Personal Event Bus (AdaptationEnacted) so PPS, AOOS, PRS, and PTOCS can react. Until then, this retrospective is filed as a record and the gap is recorded in the work map.
