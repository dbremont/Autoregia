Hypothesis · open

A short daily review catches fragmentation early

Method · hypothesis · open · 2026-06-28

Originated from Week 24: the fragmentation was only visible at the weekly review, by which point the week was already shaped. The hypothesis is that a faster feedback loop would let the correction happen inside the week.

Observation The trigger

Feedback that arrives weekly is too slow to correct within the period it describes. The control loop's feedback path is, for this class of deviation, longer than the phenomenon.

Deliberation The reasoning

A short daily review — what I intended, what I did, what I noticed — would shorten the feedback path from seven days to one. The cost is small (about five minutes); the benefit is detecting the scheduled ≠ defended pattern on day one rather than day seven.

Adaptation To be tested

Run the daily review for two weeks, then compare the weekly deviation rate against the baseline. This hypothesis is open: it has no adaptation yet because the evidence is not in. If the rate drops, the adaptation is to make the daily review a standing routine in the Reflection Practice; if it does not, the hypothesis is closed with a null result and a reason.

Adaptation → pending (hypothesis under test)

No destination yet. The proposed destination, contingent on evidence, is the Reflection Practice itself (a new standing cadence) — and, downstream, PWOS for any routine it implies.
