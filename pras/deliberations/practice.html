Reflection Practice

Method · Apex / Main Entry · Standing

This document is the apex of the reflection system. It does not record a single reflection; it states the method by which every deliberation is conducted and the path by which a settled reflection becomes an enacted adaptation. All other documents in this corpus are instances governed by this practice.

1. The Arc of a Deliberation

Every deliberation follows one movement, in three beats:

  1. Observation — what was noticed. A deviation, a pattern, a result, a surprise. Recorded before it is interpreted.
  2. Deliberation — the reasoning. Why did this happen? What does it mean against intention and policy? Held open long enough to be honest.
  3. Adaptation — the proposed change, and the system that must enact it. No deliberation closes without one.

2. The Lifecycle

A deliberation is a living object, not a finished one. It matures:

  • open — reflection in progress;
  • concluded — reasoning settled, an adaptation proposed;
  • enacted — the adaptation applied to its destination;
  • superseded — replaced by a later deliberation.

The transition concluded → enacted is the precise point at which this system feeds the policy system and the rest of the loop. Until enactment, an adaptation is only a hypothesis about how to improve.

3. Where Adaptations Feed

A concluded adaptation declares a destination — the system that must carry it out:

  • PPS — a policy, value, or commitment is amended;
  • AOOS — a corrective action or routine is registered;
  • PRS — the lesson becomes a durable record;
  • PTOCS — a capability or tool is adopted, revised, or retired.

4. Cadence

Reflection is scheduled, not waited for. The practice holds standing reviews:

  • Daily — what I intended, what I did, what I noticed.
  • Weekly — deviations of the week and the lessons chasing them.
  • Monthly / Annual — structural review of direction, identity, and the policy set.

5. Discipline

A deliberation is opened whenever the gap between intention and outcome would otherwise be lost. It is closed only when its adaptation is either enacted or explicitly declined — never left to evaporate. Reflection that does not change anything is filed as concluded with a null adaptation and a reason; drift is named, not hidden.

6. Amendment

This practice is amended only through deliberate review — and every amendment is itself recorded as a deliberation, so the method evolves by the same rules it imposes.
