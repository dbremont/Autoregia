Week 24 — the week of small interruptions

Method · review · concluded · 2026-06-21 · feeds: prs

A week where the individual tasks were fine and the shape of the week was wrong. The lesson is about defending time, not filling it.

Observation The week

  • Most planned tasks completed — throughput was not the problem.
  • Deep-work blocks fragmented four days out of five (see the deviation).
  • Two evenings lost to context-switching tax after late-afternoon interruptions.

Deliberation The pattern

The week was over-scheduled and under-defended. Every block had a task; few blocks had protection. The pattern is: a calendar entry feels like a commitment, but without policy backing it is only a suggestion, and suggestions lose to urgency.

Adaptation The change

One deviation opened and enacted (policy backing for deep-work blocks). One hypothesis opened: that a short daily review would catch fragmentation earlier.

Adaptation → concluded (filed as a record)

The week's lesson — scheduled ≠ defended — is filed as a record. The actionable part is already enacted through the deviation above; nothing further to route this cycle.
