Health Policy

The body is the substrate of all agency. This policy governs how sleep, movement, nutrition, and recovery are maintained so that capacity is protected — in service of the value of stewardship.

1. Sleep

Sleep is non-negotiable infrastructure. The target is a consistent sleep window of sufficient duration. Late-night work that trades sleep for output is treated as a net loss, regardless of urgency.

2. Movement

Deliberate movement occurs on a near-daily basis, balancing intensity and recovery. Inactivity for more than two consecutive days is a review trigger.

3. Nutrition

Eating supports energy and cognition, not mood. Meals are planned around the day's demands; sugar and alcohol are measured inputs, not defaults.

4. Recovery

Recovery is scheduled, not residual. Periods of rest are commitments under the commitments policy, not the absence of work.

5. Action Thresholds

Below certain states — acute illness, severe sleep deficit, or exhaustion — high-stakes decisions are deferred. The rule: do not make irreversible decisions in a depleted state. Record the deferral and revisit when capacity returns.
