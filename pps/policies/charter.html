Personal Charter

Identity · Main Entry · Effective 2026

This charter is the apex of my personal policy system. It states who I am, why I act, and the boundaries within which every other policy must operate. All other policy documents are subordinate to — and derive from — the principles declared here.

1. Identity

I am an agent engaged in the lifelong construction of self-management: the deliberate conversion of implicit routines into explicit, intelligible, and steerable structures. My identity is that of a practitioner of agency — one who perceives, decides, and reflects, and who takes responsibility for the coherence of those acts.

2. Purpose

The purpose of this system is to externalize perception, ideation, and reflection so that I may act with greater continuity, intelligence, and integrity than unaided memory and habit permit. Policy exists to make direction stable across mood, distraction, and time.

3. Constitution — Governing Principles

The following principles govern all subordinate policies:

  1. Explicit over implicit. Make the implicit explicit; record before relying on memory.
  2. Coherence over volume. Align action with declared values; reject what contradicts them.
  3. Commitment is binding. A commitment, once made, is altered only through deliberate review.
  4. Stewardship. Resources — time, energy, attention, money — are to be stewarded, not consumed heedlessly.

4. The Policy Set

The charter governs the following policy documents. They operationalize identity and values into concrete commitments, constraints, and directives:

  • Operating Principles — the rules of the method itself.
  • Core Values — what is non-negotiably honored.
  • Commitments — standing obligations I have taken on.
  • Health Policy — how the body is stewarded.
  • Learning Policy — how knowledge is acquired and maintained.
  • Conduct & Ethics — how I behave toward others and myself.

5. Amendment

This charter is amended only through deliberate review — never in haste, never under acute pressure. Every amendment must be traceable to a reason and dated.
