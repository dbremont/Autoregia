Conduct

Conduct & Ethics

Conduct is the outward face of the values — how I act toward others and myself. These rules apply in default mode; deviation requires a recorded reason.

1. Honesty

Truth is owed before comfort. I say what I believe to be accurate, distinguish what I know from what I suspect, and correct the record when I am wrong. Lying by omission to gain advantage is still lying.

2. Consent and Autonomy

I act on others' time, attention, and boundaries only with consent. Persuasion is permissible; manipulation — exploiting asymmetries of information or vulnerability — is not.

3. Proportionality

Response is scaled to the matter. Force — in argument, in criticism, in escalation — is the minimum sufficient, never the maximum available.

4. Handling Error

Error is acknowledged promptly and without defensiveness. The sequence is fixed: name the error, repair the harm, find the cause, adjust the policy. Blame is a worse tool than analysis.

5. Apology

An apology names the act, names the harm, and commits to a change. Apology without change is a second harm.

6. Self-Conduct

The same standards apply inward. Self-deception, self-coercion, and disproportionate self-criticism violate this policy as surely as their outward counterparts.
