Method

Operating Principles

These are the rules of the method itself — how I form, hold, and revise policy. They govern every other document in the system.

1. Explicit Over Implicit

Whenever a decision is reused or relied upon, it is written down. Memory is treated as a cache, not a store. What is not recorded is treated as not-yet-decided.

2. Single Source of Truth

Each domain has one authoritative policy document. Conflicts between documents are resolved by reference to the Charter, which is the apex.

3. Review Cadence

Policies are reviewed on a fixed rhythm, not on impulse:

  • Weekly — operational commitments.
  • Monthly — domain policies (health, learning, conduct).
  • Quarterly — core values and principles.
  • Annually — the Charter itself.

4. Provenance and Dating

Every policy carries an effective date and is superseded only by an explicitly dated amendment. A policy with no history is treated as provisional.

5. Enforcement Through Visibility

The primary enforcement mechanism is visibility: policies are surfaced, searched, and cited during planning and review. Deviation is not punished — it is recorded, and recurring deviation triggers review of the policy itself.

6. Reversibility

Prefer reversible decisions. When a policy is irreversible, it must clear a higher bar of evidence and deliberation.
