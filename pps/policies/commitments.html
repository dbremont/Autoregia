Commitments

A commitment is a standing obligation. Once made, it alters only through deliberate review. This document lists the classes of commitment I hold and the rule under which they are revised.

The Binding Principle

Per the Charter, commitment is binding. A new obligation does not silently displace an old one; capacity must be accounted for before a commitment is accepted.

Classes of Commitment

Relational

Obligations to family, friends, and collaborators. These hold the highest priority and are revised least often. Breaking one requires notice and, where possible, renegotiation.

Project

Obligations tied to specific deliverables. Each carries a scope, a deadline, and an exit criterion. A project commitment is retired when its exit criterion is met or explicitly rescinded.

Recurring Duties

Standing routines — reviews, maintenance, reporting. These are scheduled and protected; skipping a recurring duty is a signal, not a failure, and triggers examination of the duty itself.

Capacity Rule

No commitment is accepted without confirming capacity. The current load is visible in the PRS (Accounting) and the task system; over-commitment is treated as a policy violation of stewardship.

Revision

Commitments are reviewed weekly. To change one: name it, name the reason, name the consequence, and date it. Silent abandonment is forbidden.
